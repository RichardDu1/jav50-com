## Overview

Frame interpolation creates new frames between existing ones, turning a fast time-lapse into smooth slow-motion. This guide uses a combination of DaVinci Resolve for editing, Topaz Video AI for high-quality interpolation, and FlowFrames for a free alternative.

## Step 1: Prepare Your Time-Lapse Footage

- Import your time-lapse video into DaVinci Resolve.
- Identify the key moments where you want slow-motion.
- Cut the timeline at those points using the Blade tool (B).

## Step 2: Export Segments for Interpolation

- Select each segment you want to slow down.
- Go to **Deliver** page, choose **Individual Clips**.
- Format: **QuickTime** with **ProRes 422** (or **PNG sequence** for best quality).
- Export each segment separately.

## Step 3: Interpolate Frames with Topaz Video AI

- Open Topaz Video AI.
- Drag in your exported segment.
- Under **AI Model**, select **Chronos** (best for slow-motion).
- Set **Frame Interpolation** to **2x** or **4x** (higher = smoother but more processing).
- Enable **Motion Deblur** if needed.
- Click **Start** to process.
- Export as **ProRes** or **PNG sequence**.

## Step 4: Alternative Free Method – FlowFrames

- Download FlowFrames (Windows only, uses RIFE AI).
- Drag your segment into the app.
- Set **Interpolation Factor** (e.g., 2x).
- Click **Start**.
- Output will be an MP4; you can re-encode to ProRes later.

## Step 5: Reassemble in DaVinci Resolve

- Import the interpolated slow-motion clips back into your timeline.
- Replace the original segments with the new clips.
- Adjust timing: if you used 2x interpolation, the clip will be twice as long. Trim as needed.
- Add speed ramps for dramatic effect: right-click clip → **Change Clip Speed** → use keyframes to transition from fast to slow.

## Step 6: Final Render

- Go to **Deliver** page.
- Choose **Master** settings: **H.264** for web, **ProRes** for archiving.
- Click **Add to Render Queue** and **Start Render**.

## Tips

- For best results, shoot time-lapse at a higher frame rate (e.g., 30fps) to give interpolation more data.
- Use **Optical Flow** in DaVinci Resolve as a quick alternative (right-click clip → **Retime Controls** → **Optical Flow**), but AI tools yield smoother results.
- Test with a short clip first to dial in settings.

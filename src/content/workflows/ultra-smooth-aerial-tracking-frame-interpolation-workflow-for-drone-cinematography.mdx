## Overview
Frame interpolation creates new frames between existing ones, doubling or tripling your drone footage's frame rate for ultra-smooth motion. This workflow is ideal for aerial tracking shots where camera movement is fast or erratic.

## Prerequisites
- **Hardware**: NVIDIA RTX 3060 or higher (RTX 4090 recommended for real-time preview)
- **Software**: DaVinci Resolve 18 (free), Topaz Video AI (paid), Flowframes (free)
- **Footage**: 4K 30fps or 60fps drone clips with minimal motion blur

## Step 1: Capture Optimal Source Footage
- Shoot at 60fps (or 30fps if limited) with a shutter speed of 1/120s (180° rule).
- Use smooth gimbal movements; avoid sudden jerks.
- Export as ProRes 422 or H.264 high bitrate.

## Step 2: Preprocess in DaVinci Resolve
1. Import footage into a new timeline (set to 60fps).
2. Apply color grading and stabilization (if needed) before interpolation.
3. Render out a ProRes 422 clip for interpolation.

## Step 3: Interpolate with Topaz Video AI (Recommended)
1. Open Topaz Video AI and load the clip.
2. Choose model: **Chronos** for high quality, **Apollo** for speed.
3. Set output frame rate: 120fps (2x) or 240fps (4x).
4. Enable **Motion Deblur** if footage is slightly blurry.
5. Render to ProRes 422. (GPU: RTX 4090 renders 4K 30fps to 120fps in ~5 min per minute of footage.)

## Step 4: Alternative Free Option – Flowframes
1. Install Flowframes and download the RIFE model.
2. Drag clip into Flowframes, set interpolation factor (e.g., 2x).
3. Use **SCUNet** denoise if needed.
4. Render to MP4. (Slower than Topaz but free.)

## Step 5: Final Assembly in DaVinci Resolve
1. Import interpolated clip into a 120fps timeline.
2. Adjust speed to 50% for slow-motion or keep at 100% for smooth real-time.
3. Add subtle motion blur (0.5-1.0 shutter angle) for cinematic feel.
4. Export as 4K 60fps H.264 for delivery.

## Tips for Best Results
- Avoid interpolating footage with large occlusions or fast-moving objects.
- Use **Optical Flow** in DaVinci Resolve as a quick alternative (but lower quality).
- For real-time preview, use a GPU with at least 12GB VRAM.

## Conclusion
This workflow delivers professional-grade smooth aerial shots, reducing the need for expensive high-frame-rate drones. With Topaz Video AI, you can achieve 4x interpolation in minutes, saving hours of manual work.
